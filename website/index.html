01 · About

What is PriceHarvest?

Agricultural crop prices fluctuate due to seasonal variations, supply-demand dynamics, and regional market factors. Understanding these patterns is critical for farmers, traders, and policymakers.

PriceHarvest analyzes three years of historical mandi price data from the Agmarknet Portal, Government of India to uncover seasonal patterns, long-term trends, and regional pricing behavior.

📊

Exploratory Analysis

Price distributions, outliers, statistical summaries, histograms, box plots, KDE curves, and market comparison charts across all 3 crops and both states.

📈

Trend Identification

Time-series decomposition into trend, seasonal, and residual components. Rolling averages (7/14/30 day) and 30-day linear forecast.

🗺️

Regional Comparison

Side-by-side Haryana vs Maharashtra analysis. Violin plots, seasonal heatmaps, and volatility comparison reveal major regional pricing differences.

💡

Price Intelligence

Auto-generated narrative insights engine — seasonal peaks, volatility rankings, market anomalies, and cross-state pricing gaps summarized clearly.

05 · Key Insights

What the Data Reveals

01

Tomato is the Most Volatile

Prices spiked to ₹12,000/Qtl in July 2023 — driven by heat damage and monsoon transport disruptions.

CV = 71.8% · Mumbai
02

Wheat is the Most Stable

Government MSP support and controlled procurement keep Wheat at the lowest volatility of all crops.

CV = 5.4% · Both States
03

Mumbai Wheat Costs 84% More

Maharashtra wheat averages ₹4,273/Qtl vs Haryana's ₹2,324 — long-distance transport and market margins.

₹2,324 vs ₹4,273
04

Onion Peaks Every November

Consistently highest in November (post-Kharif) and lowest in April-May, following the Rabi harvest cycle.

Best month: November
05

Monsoon Pushes Tomato High

Transport disruptions drive Tomato to annual highs in July–August, before cooling in October–November harvest.

Peak: July–August
06

Min–Modal Highly Correlated

Correlation r = 0.94 between Min and Modal price — most trading happens near the minimum quoted price on thin-supply mandi days.

r = 0.94 correlation
07

🍅 Tomato — Best & Worst Months

Haryana peaks in August (~₹38.5/kg) and dips in May (~₹8.6/kg). Mumbai peaks in July (~₹53.2/kg) and is cheapest in April (~₹13.1/kg).

Aug · Haryana  |  Jul · Mumbai
08

🧅 Onion — Best & Worst Months

Both states peak in November — Haryana (~₹25.4/kg) and Mumbai (~₹30.3/kg) — and bottom out in April–May after Rabi harvest.

Nov · Both States
09

🌾 Wheat — Best & Worst Months

Haryana peaks in April (~₹23.3/kg). Mumbai peaks in November (~₹43.7/kg). Wheat stays stable year-round due to MSP — the difference between best and worst is under ₹2/kg.

Apr · Haryana  |  Nov · Mumbai
